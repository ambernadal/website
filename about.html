Welcome! I am originally from Orange County, California. I attended Point Loma Nazarene University in San Diego, CA as an undergraduate, where I earned a B.A. in Therapeutic and Community Psychology. Currently, I am a fourth-year Social Psychology doctoral student at Brigham Young University in Provo, Utah where I work closely with my advisor, Sam Hardy.

Over the last several years, I have become increasingly interested in how virtue ethics informs the study of moral traits, with a particular interest in the judgement-action gap. Moving forward, I am looking for patterns in within-person variability to describe character types that are more or less likely to experience discrepancies between their values and actions. For more information, please visit my research page.

When I am not researching, I like to rock climb, rollerblade, read books about the future of technology, build epic forts with my son, and have theoretical conversations (mostly about metaphysics and morality). I also like to travel, and am lucky enough to have been to over 25 countries.

Please feel free to email me if you have any questions about my research, teaching, or tutorials!
