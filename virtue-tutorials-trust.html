Trust

The exercise below is based on the standard ABC model of cognitive-behavioral therapy. Generally, the key to this exercise is challenging beliefs that lead us to have negative feelings or consequences. Although this is more commonly thought of as a positive thinking exercise, I believe that it taps into trust of others quite well.

Activating Event
Describe a recent situation which led you to feel frustrated, hurt, or to otherwise experience adversity "e.g. my friend never returned my call").

*

    Beliefs
    Describe your beliefs and/or interpretation of that event ("e.g. my friend must not value our friendship very much").

    *

      Consequences
      Describe the feelings that you have as a result of what you believe about the activating event("e.g. I feel hurt and abandoned").

      *

        Disputing Beliefs
        Challenge your beliefs listed above. Frequently, the beliefs that lead us to negative emotional consequences are unrealistic. Challenge those beliefs with beliefs that are more plausible ("e.g. my friend must be really busy right now").

        *

          New Feelings
          If you adopt this disputing belief in place of your old one, how do you now feel about the event ("e.g. I feel secure and patient in my friendship, and am eager to catch up")?

          *

            *Double click on added items to remove them from the list.
